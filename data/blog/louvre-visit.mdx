## "The Dutch Tippler" and "The Dutch Appler"

![https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c8/Gabri%C3%ABl_Metsu_-_Woman_Peeling_an_Apple_-_WGA15084.jpg](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c8/Gabri%C3%ABl_Metsu_-_Woman_Peeling_an_Apple_-_WGA15084.jpg)

Gabriel Metsu's paintings, "The Dutch Tippler" and "The Dutch Appler," represent two sides of the same coin and convey the influence of Calvinist indoctrination during the time. In "The Dutch Tippler," a woman holding a jug of wine symbolizes overindulgence, portraying the negative view. On the other hand, "The Dutch Appler" presents a positive perspective. The apple woman delicately peels an apple, its spiraling peel representing the passage of time. She understands the transient nature of life and recognizes the impermanence of indulgences.

## Interior of a Dutch House

![https://www.repro-tableaux.com/kunst/pieter_de_hooch/Interior-of-a-Dutch-House.jpg](https://www.repro-tableaux.com/kunst/pieter_de_hooch/Interior-of-a-Dutch-House.jpg)

Pieter de Hooch's painting, "Interior of a Dutch House," depicts a solemn household where the significance of duties is paramount. Food preparation in this context symbolizes the exchange between the living and the dead, particularly through representations of meat. The focus of the painting lies not on abundance but on sobriety and virtuous living. Interestingly, the door in the painting not only provides a view into the household but also plays with perspective, revealing the deeper structure of the house.

## The Vegetable Market

![https://www.artnet.com/WebServices/images/ll00157lldJ3uGFgNKECfDrCWvaHBOcD1JD/gabriel-metsu-a-vegetable-market-in-amsterdam.jpg](https://www.artnet.com/WebServices/images/ll00157lldJ3uGFgNKECfDrCWvaHBOcD1JD/gabriel-metsu-a-vegetable-market-in-amsterdam.jpg)

Gabriel Metsu's "The Vegetable Market" showcases the livelier atmosphere of market scenes compared to the home kitchen setting. In the foreground, an assortment of vegetables and a chicken catch the eye. However, amidst the vibrant scene, there is a hint of disagreement over the price of the vegetables, adding a touch of tension to the painting.

## The Dutch Cook

![https://cdn.gallerix.asia/sr/_UNK/1014246250/276091298.jpg](https://cdn.gallerix.asia/sr/_UNK/1014246250/276091298.jpg)

"A Dutch Cook" captures a dark kitchen filled with a variety of vegetables, prominently featuring carrots. The presence of a flirtatious kitchen maid suggests a potential sexual relationship symbolized by the carrots. This painting explores the interplay between desire and sensuality within the culinary realm.

## Slaughtered Ox

![https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Slaughtered_ox_Rembrandt.jpg/1200px-Slaughtered_ox_Rembrandt.jpg](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Slaughtered_ox_Rembrandt.jpg/1200px-Slaughtered_ox_Rembrandt.jpg)

Rembrandt's painting, "Slaughtered Ox," employs loose brush strokes to portray the carcass as something grotesque, evoking a sense of tragedy rather than wealth. The detailed depiction underscores the transient and fragile nature of life, urging viewers to contemplate the fragility of their existence.

## A Feast of Kings

![https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/8f/Jordaens_King_Drinks_1638-40.jpg](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/8f/Jordaens_King_Drinks_1638-40.jpg)

Jakob Jordaens' work, "A Feast of the Kings," presents a scene of both gluttony and familial love. The painting depicts a custom where a small bean is hidden in a tart, determining the "king" for the evening, who then names their court members. Amidst the indulgence and merriment, the room is filled with joy, emphasizing the sense of shared celebration.

## The Ray

![https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/La_Raie_-_Jean_Baptiste_Sim%C3%A9on_Chardin_-_Mus%C3%A9e_du_Louvre_Peintures_INV_3197.jpg/1200px-La_Raie_-_Jean_Baptiste_Sim%C3%A9on_Chardin_-_Mus%C3%A9e_du_Louvre_Peintures_INV_3197.jpg](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/La_Raie_-_Jean_Baptiste_Sim%C3%A9on_Chardin_-_Mus%C3%A9e_du_Louvre_Peintures_INV_3197.jpg/1200px-La_Raie_-_Jean_Baptiste_Sim%C3%A9on_Chardin_-_Mus%C3%A9e_du_Louvre_Peintures_INV_3197.jpg)

Chardonne's painting, "The Ray," features a sorrowful and grotesque portrayal of a butchered animal, with its intestines flowing out. Within the ray, a human face evokes a sense of empathy from the viewer. Notably, this painting breaks conventions by showcasing a still life at a large scale, a departure from the typical reserved status of large-scale paintings for biblical or mythological scenes. Through this work, viewers witness the transformation of food and contemplate the processes of life and death.
