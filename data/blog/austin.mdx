## Introduction

If you're looking for a fun summer getaway, Austin, Texas is the perfect destination. Known for its eclectic music scene, delicious food, and vibrant culture, Austin has something for everyone. In this blog post, we'll explore three must-see attractions to add to your itinerary for a summer visit to Austin.

## Activities

### Barton Springs Pool

One of the best ways to beat the summer heat is by visiting Barton Springs Pool. This natural spring-fed pool is a refreshing oasis in the heart of the city. The water is always a cool 68 degrees, making it a popular spot for locals and visitors alike. The pool is three acres in size and has plenty of space to swim, lounge, and relax. You can also rent a paddleboard or kayak and explore the spring-fed creek that feeds into the pool. With plenty of trees and shade around the pool, it's the perfect spot to spend a summer afternoon in Austin.

### South Congress Avenue / Dirty Sixth

![BartonSpringsPool](https://img.texasmonthly.com/2022/03/south-congress-austin-texas.jpg?auto=compress&crop=faces&fit=fit&fm=pjpg&ixlib=php-3.3.1&q=45)

South Congress Avenue, also known as SoCo, is a vibrant street in Austin that's full of unique shops, restaurants, and bars. You'll find everything from vintage clothing boutiques to gourmet food trucks along this bustling street. SoCo is also home to some of Austin's most famous murals, including the "I love you so much" wall and the "Greetings from Austin" mural. Grab a coffee at Jo's Coffee and snap a photo in front of the iconic "I love you so much" wall before exploring the rest of SoCo.

### Congress Avenue Bridge Bats

Another unique Austin experience is watching the Congress Avenue Bridge Bats. Every evening from March to October, over 1.5 million bats emerge from underneath the Congress Avenue Bridge in downtown Austin. It's the largest urban bat colony in North America and a sight to behold. You can watch the bats from the bridge or take a boat tour to see them up close. Watching the bats is a quintessential Austin experience that you won't want to miss.

### Texas Hill Country

If you're looking to escape the city for a day trip, take a drive out to Texas Hill Country. This region is known for its picturesque landscapes, wineries, and charming small towns. You can go hiking or biking in the hills, take a tour of a local winery, or simply enjoy the scenic drive. Some popular towns to visit in Texas Hill Country include Fredericksburg, Wimberley, and Dripping Springs. You can also visit the famous Hamilton Pool Preserve, a natural swimming hole with a stunning waterfall.

## Food and Drink

![TorchyTacos](https://ilovetheburg.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/torchys-tacos.png)

No trip to Austin is complete without indulging in the local food and drink scene. Austin is known for its barbecue, Tex-Mex, and craft beer. Some popular barbecue spots include Franklin Barbecue, La Barbecue, and Terry Black's Barbecue. For Tex-Mex, head to places like Torchy's Tacos, Trudy's, or Matt's El Rancho. You can also find plenty of craft breweries and cocktail bars throughout the city. Some popular spots include Jester King Brewery, The White Horse, and The Roosevelt Room.

## Conclusion

Whether you're looking to explore the city or escape to the countryside, a summer visit to Austin is sure to be a memorable experience. From swimming in Barton Springs Pool to watching the Congress Avenue Bridge Bats, there's something for everyone in this vibrant city. Be sure to indulge in the local food and drink scene and take a day trip to Texas Hill Country for some stunning scenery. With so much to see and do, you'll be planning your next trip to Austin before you know it.
